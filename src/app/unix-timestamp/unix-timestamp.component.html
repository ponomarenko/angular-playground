About Unix Timestamp

A Unix timestamp represents time as a continuous count of seconds starting from the Unix Epoch (January 1, 1970, 00:00:00 UTC). This format remains consistent across all time zones, making it an essential tool for computer systems to track and organize time-based data in both online and offline applications.

Common Time Intervals:

Time Period Seconds
Hour 3,600
Day 86,400
Week 604,800
Month (30.44 days) 2,629,743
Year (365.24 days) 31,556,926

Important Note

On January 19, 2038, the computing world will face the "Year 2038 Problem" - a moment when 32-bit systems will reach their maximum Unix timestamp value. To address this limitation, systems must transition to 64-bit time storage formats, which will significantly extend the timestamp's usable range.
